About the Bohr Model

The Bohr model shows the arrangement of electrons in shells around the nucleus. Each shell can hold a specific maximum number of electrons. The model is a simplified representation and doesn't account for the wave nature of electrons described by quantum mechanics, but it's useful for visualizing atomic structure.
